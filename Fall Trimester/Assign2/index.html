Transformation of a Photo

By Nick Keirstead

October 24, 2013

The purpose of this assignment in computer science class was, primarily, to learn about photoshop and its powers of manipulation. We had to start with an image that we either got from the internet or took ourselves, and then perform a series of edits on it. It was important to keep track of the exact steps we took and what order they were completed in, as we had to list them on one of our webpages (there are two this time!).

We also learned new html skills, especially concerning how to display photographs with the img tag. As well, chapter 3 of Blown to Bits taught us about meta-data, and how it can give you information about when, how, and even where a photograph was taken. So without further ado...

Brief Description:

This image is from the center of campus at Horace Mann School, in Bronx, NY. It was taken on a late summer afternoon, depicting a clear sky and the field all setup for soccer preseason. Fisher Hall is the building with the clock tower in the distance, while to the right of it is Phorzheimer Hall. Remember what this scene looks like, for it will change a bit on the next page.
